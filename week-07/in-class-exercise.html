In-class exercise

Please respect the time limits. Of course you do not need to set a timer, but it is important that you make it to the very end.

Task 1: Debugging (30min)

  • Go to https://classroom.github.com/g/LEkqDQY2 to set up a repository for your group as usual.

  • You will find a file cobb_douglas.py in that repository. This script contains several errors.

  • Look together for the bugs using Live Share. Once you think you encountered a bug, discuss your hypothesis with your fellow group members. Use the symbolic debugger to step verify your hypothesis.

  • Feel free to add additional numbers to be calculated, if that helps.

  • Stop your fellow group members from doing anything if they do not use the scientific method!

  • Hint: The correct output should be:

    3.4739534644393735
2.933503810642718

  • Note: Some of the bugs may not be subtle enough to require lots of thought. But do discuss your hypothesis in any case before you change anything.

  • Commit your changes when you are done. You may of course commit earlier versions, too.

Task 2: Pythonic functional constructs: List comprehensions (20min)

Continue to work in cobb_douglas.py.

  • Replace the for-loop where the calculations are done by a list comprehension.
  • Replace the line doing the printing by print(*results, sep="\n"). The asterisk is doing argument unpacking, that is, instead of the list results it passes the list’s elements to the print() function. Note: If you feel like reading up on this and you find an accessible reference, please let us know about that (anything we found quickly became technical). But do not worry too much, this is not of crucial importance in this course.
  • Commit once you are finished.

Task 3: Pythonic functional constructs: List comprehensions (20min)

You want to identify the two items in the input data by something else than their order in a list.

  • Think of two names of people identifying the two sets of measures.
  • Re-write the statement defining the inputs so that inputs will be a dictionary.
  • Re-write the list comprehension defining the results as a dict comprehension, which iterates over inputs.items().
  • Leave the line printing the result as it is and see what happens. Then change it to print(*results, sep="\n") and explain to each other what happens.
