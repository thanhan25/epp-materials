Effective Programming Practices for Economists, Winter Term 2020/21

Universität Bonn, Department of Economics

Prof. Dr. Hans-Martin von Gaudecker

Learning Objectives

Methods

  • Students explain basic concepts of graph theory
  • Students recognise some important types of graphs (tree, arborescence, DAG)
  • Students relate graph theory to the file system on their computer
  • Students appreciate that a sequence of typed commands is more amenable to being reproducible than a sequence of clicks / swishes.

Tools

  • Students navigate the directory tree in the Shell
  • Students create, view, and rename files in the Shell
  • Students regularly use tab completion in the Shell

Personal

  • Students are not discouraged by a wide variety of introductory material.
  • Students manage their time well so they complete all required tasks on time.
  • Students reflect on their own contribution to group work along the dimensions of responsibility, thinking abilities, cooperation, and autonomy & initiative

Social

  • Students discuss the most important dimensions of responsibility and thinking abilities
  • Students rate their peers’ contribution to group work along the dimensions of responsibility, thinking abilities, cooperation, and autonomy & initiative
  • Students help each other in making sure that all group members get started on the first assignment and everybody knows what to do
