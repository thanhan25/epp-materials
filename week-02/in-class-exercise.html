In-class exercise

Please respect the time limits. Of course you do not need to set a timer, but it is important that you make it to the very end.

Task 1: Pentabilities (20min)

Please discuss the following questions with your group and come up with an answer. There are no right / wrong answers and you do not necessarily need to converge to an opinion! The important bit is to think about the concepts and to discuss them.

  1. Which of the 5 pentabilities – Responsibility, Cooperation, Autonomy and Initiative, Emotion Management and Thinking abilities – do you think can be observed easiest?

    1. Responsibility
    2. Cooperation
    3. Autonomy and Initiative
    4. Emotion Management
    5. Thinking abilities

  2. We will start using the Pentabilities system along two of the five dimensions today: Responsibility and Thinking abilities. To start familiarising yourselves with the concrete behaviors Pentabilities associates with these concepts, discuss the meaning of the behaviours among the group members. Then choose the two that sound most relevant to you.

    Responsibility

    • Works in a constant manner
    • Stays connected to the group’s activity
    • Make comments or engages in activities related to the task at hand
    • Performs the tasks efficiently
    • Performs the tasks carefully
    • Perseveres in face of difficulties
    • Respects the rules

    Thinking abilities

    • Relates new content with previous knowledge
    • Makes good reflections on the content
    • Makes good reflections on internal personal processes
    • Asks good questions (to improve understanding or to move forward)
    • Has creative ideas (in the sense that it explores alternative paths)
    • Proposes good strategies for problem solving
    • Willing to discover different perspectives
    • Expresses ideas effectively (correction, precision and structure)

    Once we let you know that everything is set up, please go through the steps “Getting started with the App” in the Pentabilities document we shared with you during the past week.

Task 2: Relation between graphs and the file system (10min)

  • Explain how the file system on your computer can be seen as a graph.
  • Is it directed or undirected?
  • What is the graph’s topology (tree / arborescence / DAG / …)?
  • Is it just one connected graph or several connected graphs?

Please be prepared to answer these questions in a short plenary session.

Task 3: Shell (10min)

  • Find two things when you would want to use a shell rather than a GUI! (or do you think we presented it just for historic reasons?)

  • Name two tasks where you definitely do not want to use a shell.

Please be prepared to answer these questions in a short plenary session.
