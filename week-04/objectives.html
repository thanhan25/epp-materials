Effective Programming Practices for Economists, Winter Term 2020/21

Universität Bonn, Department of Economics

Prof. Dr. Hans-Martin von Gaudecker

Learning Objectives

Methods

  • Students understand the basic ideas
  • Students explain the benefits and pitfalls of mutable objects
  • Students explain using an example what “state” means in programming
  • Students explain the importance of minimising state when coding
  • Students apply the basic principles of functional programming
  • Students appreciate the importance of parsing through error messages for learning

Tools

  • Students define functions in Python
  • In any particular application, students choose the right basic data type in Python among
    • Booleans
    • Integers
    • Floating Point numbers
    • Strings
  • In any particular application, students choose the right basic container type in Python among
    • Lists
    • Tuples
    • Sets
    • Dictionaries
  • Students apply the following functions in Python
    • map
    • filter
    • reduce

Personal

  • Students are not discouraged by seeing errors they do not understand
  • Students describe bugs precisely to their peers and the teachers
  • Students reflect on their own contribution to group work along the dimensions of the Pentabilities

Social

  • Students discuss the most important dimensions of emotion management
  • Students rate their peers’ contribution to group work along the dimensions of the Pentabilities
  • Students discuss Python syntax and the concept of mutability
  • Students jointly search for solutions to bugs and develop the language to communicate about them
